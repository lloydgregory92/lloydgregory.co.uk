My experiences so far.

Metafour UK

I began working at Metafour in November 2014, starting as a Junior Developer I quickly established myself within the company. I am now one of the main UK developers, also UK Test Coordinator & Client Champion are roles created especially for me. During my time at Metafour I have developed an automated testing suite, it's purpose is to perform regression tests on various modules of our biggest product. Since taking on the role of UK Test Coordinator I believe that the testing standard has increased, and now works more efficiently. I have also worked on implementations & customer demos helping the Sales & Marketing teams. I also contirbute to product support, dealing with day to day issues from our Clients.

Cardiff City Stadium

I began working at Cardiff City Stadium late 2010. It began as a part-time job so I would have some sort of income . My position at the Stadium quickly grew and was promoted to a hospitality team leader after only a few shifts. Following that I was then given the opportunity to become a Hospitality supervisor, a role that required me to take charge of a team of 20-25 member of staff, handling the service of around 2,000 guests per match. Although not connected to my field of study I believe the experienced gained at the Stadium gave me a better standing when applying for different positions.

Private Applied ICT Tutor

I began tutoring a sixth-form pupil who was studiyng Applied ICT. I covered both Exam and Course work lessons, providing documentation which I saw useful and relevant for the teaching. I spent 2 nights a week with Josh prior to his final Course Work submission and Exam sittings, Josh felt he needed a little extra help after recieving lower predicted grades than he was expecting. Following my tutorage Josh's grades improved and he passed his course, He is now studying at University.

Teacher Assistant - Volunteer

I spent two weeks volunteering at my old Comprehensive school, helping teach IT subjects. Taking classes from form 7 up to sixth form. Covering a range of topics from basic IT to web design and Visual Basic programming. It was such a rewarding experience, following the two weeks I began looking into doing teaching qualifications and was convinced that I wanted to become a teacher. The only thing that kept me from following it was the idea of not developing my development skills, while teaching consists of following a syllabus and a lot of repetition, there would be little time for my own Software Development.
